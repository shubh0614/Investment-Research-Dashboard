# NVIDIA Corporation - Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call Summary

**Date:** November 20, 2024
**Source:** NVIDIA Investor Relations
**Document Type:** Earnings Call Summary
**Ticker:** NVDA

## Financial Highlights

| Metric | Q3 FY2025 | Q3 FY2024 | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue | $35.1B | $18.1B | +94% |
| Data Center Revenue | $30.8B | $14.5B | +112% |
| Gaming Revenue | $3.3B | $2.9B | +15% |
| Gross Margin (GAAP) | 74.6% | 70.0% | +460bps |
| EPS (Non-GAAP diluted) | $0.81 | $0.40 | +103% |

**Q4 FY2025 Guidance:** Revenue ~$37.5B ± 2%

## CEO Commentary (Jensen Huang)

"Demand for Hopper remains very strong while preparations for Blackwell ramp are progressing. We are now shipping Blackwell in full production. Our data center customers are building AI factories - not just AI servers."

Key themes from the call:
- Blackwell architecture shipping at scale; GB200 NVL72 systems deployed in customer data centers
- Hyperscaler demand (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) driven by AI training and inference workloads
- Sovereign AI investments accelerating in India, Japan, Southeast Asia
- CUDA ecosystem supports over 4 million developers; network effects create durable moat

## Business Segment Analysis

### Data Center (88% of revenue)
- Hopper H100/H200 still the primary revenue driver; Blackwell ramp beginning
- Inference demand outpacing training for the first time; multiple customers cite 40-60% inference share
- NVLink networking revenue growing; InfiniBand market share expanding vs. Ethernet
- Key customers: Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, AWS, Meta, Oracle Cloud

### Gaming (9% of revenue)
- RTX 40-series refresh cycle continuing; RTX 5000 launch on the horizon
- AI-powered features (DLSS 3.5, Frame Generation) differentiating from AMD

### Professional Visualization (2% of revenue)
- Recovery continuing; enterprise AI workstation demand growing

## Risk Factors

1. **Supply chain concentration:** CoWoS advanced packaging at TSMC; single-source risk for HBM (SK Hynix)
2. **Export controls:** U.S. BIS restrictions on H20 exports to China reduced FY2025 revenue by ~$2.0B
3. **Competition:** AMD MI300X gaining hyperscaler traction; Google TPU v5 reducing captive demand
4. **Customer concentration:** Top 5 hyperscalers account for ~45% of Data Center revenue
5. **Valuation risk:** 35x forward P/E leaves limited margin for execution shortfalls
6. **Cyclicality:** Prior AI infrastructure cycles (crypto) show demand can reverse quickly

## Analyst Consensus

- Consensus price target: $165 (range $120–$200)
- 35 Buy / 8 Hold / 2 Sell ratings
- Primary bull thesis: Blackwell supercycle + inference scaling laws extend upgrade cycle through 2026
- Primary bear thesis: Hyperscaler CapEx discipline reassertion; AMD/custom-silicon market-share erosion
