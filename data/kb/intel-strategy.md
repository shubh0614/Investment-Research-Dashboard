# Intel Corporation — Strategic Transformation Update 2024

**Source:** Intel Investor Day 2024 + Q3 2024 Earnings Call
**Document Type:** Strategic Analysis
**Ticker:** INTC

## Transformation Overview

Intel is executing a four-pillar turnaround under CEO Pat Gelsinger:

1. **IDM 2.0** — Retain internal manufacturing (vs. going fabless) while opening fabs to external customers
2. **Intel Foundry Services (IFS)** — Compete with TSMC and Samsung for external chip orders
3. **Process Technology Recovery** — Catch up to TSMC on node competitiveness (Intel 18A targeting 2025)
4. **Product Portfolio Refresh** — Regain data center CPU leadership; build AI accelerator business (Gaudi)

## Process Technology Roadmap

| Node | Equivalent TSMC | Status | Key Products |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intel 7 | TSMC N7 | In production | Sapphire Rapids, Meteor Lake |
| Intel 4 | TSMC N5 | In production | Meteor Lake, Arrow Lake |
| Intel 3 | TSMC N3 | Production 2024 | Panther Lake |
| Intel 18A | TSMC N2 | Tape-in 2024, volume 2025 | Clearwater Forest |
| Intel 14A | TSMC A16 | Planning stage | Post-2025 |

Intel 18A is considered the critical inflection. If it achieves claimed PPA (power-performance-area) metrics, Intel becomes competitive with TSMC for the first time since 2016.

## Gaudi AI Accelerator

### Gaudi 3 Specifications
- Peak BF16: 1,835 TFLOPS (spec claim; workload-dependent actual performance)
- HBM capacity: 128GB HBM2e
- Price point: ~30-40% below H100 at list price
- Key differentiator: Open software stack (OpenAI Triton-compatible), no proprietary API lock-in

### Market Traction
- Gaudi 3 revenue contribution "immaterial" in FY2024
- SAP, Dell, Lenovo announced Gaudi 3 systems
- Intel targets $500M Gaudi revenue in FY2025 (vs. NVIDIA's $30B+)
- Primary addressable market: enterprises skeptical of NVIDIA pricing + software lock-in

### Software Challenge
Gaudi uses OpenAPI for compute but customers accustomed to CUDA must migrate. Intel's pitch is avoidance of lock-in, but without a developer ecosystem, migration friction is high.

## Financial Position

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024E |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $54.2B | ~$53.5B |
| Gross Margin | 44.8% | ~38% (declining during fab investment) |
| Operating Loss | -$1.7B | ~-$5.0B (peak investment year) |
| CapEx | $25.0B | ~$25.0B |
| Free Cash Flow | -$13.9B | Negative |

Intel is deliberately running at a loss to fund the fab ramp. Management calls FY2025-2026 the "trough years" before IFS revenue and 18A product benefits accrue.

## Key Risks

1. **18A execution risk:** Any delay or yield problem extends the competitive gap vs. TSMC. Two prior node delays (Intel 7, Intel 4) damaged customer confidence.
2. **IFS customer acquisition:** Microsoft is the anchor external customer (committed to Intel 18A). Getting a second major hyperscaler customer is critical for IFS viability.
3. **Data center CPU erosion:** AMD EPYC has captured ~30% of server CPU market; Intel Sapphire Rapids lost on performance/watt. Granite Rapids (Intel 3) must reverse this.
4. **Cash burn:** At -$14B FCF, Intel has limited margin for execution errors before needing to raise capital or sell assets.
5. **Talent:** Multiple senior engineers departed during the turnaround; rebuilding process engineering expertise takes years.
6. **Geopolitical risk:** Intel's Ohio and Germany fabs are receiving government subsidies (CHIPS Act, EU Chips Act). Political changes could affect subsidy timelines.

## Bull / Bear Summary

**Bull case:** 18A executes on schedule, IFS lands 2-3 more hyperscaler customers, Gaudi gains traction in price-sensitive segments, data center CPU stabilizes with Granite Rapids. Sum-of-parts valuation with IFS at TSMC multiples yields $60-80/share.

**Bear case:** 18A yields disappointing, TSMC lead extends to 2+ generations, IFS remains a cost center, AMD completes server CPU share capture. Revenue declines to $45B by 2026; stock revisits $20.
